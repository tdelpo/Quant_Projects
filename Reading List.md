Roadmap to Quant Trading:

𝟭. 𝗨𝗻𝗱𝗲𝗿𝘀𝘁𝗮𝗻𝗱 𝗵𝗼𝘄 𝗺𝗮𝗿𝗸𝗲𝘁𝘀 𝗮𝗰𝘁𝘂𝗮𝗹𝗹𝘆 𝘄𝗼𝗿𝗸
Before strategies, understand the battlefield.

- 𝗧𝗿𝗮𝗱𝗶𝗻𝗴 𝗮𝗻𝗱 𝗘𝘅𝗰𝗵𝗮𝗻𝗴𝗲𝘀 𝗯𝘆 𝗟𝗮𝗿𝗿𝘆 𝗛𝗮𝗿𝗿𝗶𝘀
  This book changed how I think about markets. Liquidity, order flow, spreads, execution, market participants… things most beginners ignore while jumping straight into alpha.

And for pure inspiration:

- 𝗧𝗵𝗲 𝗠𝗮𝗻 𝗪𝗵𝗼 𝗦𝗼𝗹𝘃𝗲𝗱 𝘁𝗵𝗲 𝗠𝗮𝗿𝗸𝗲𝘁
  Probably one of the most exciting books to understand why quant finance became such a big deal. Hard to read it and not get obsessed with the field.

𝟮. 𝗟𝗲𝗮𝗿𝗻 𝗵𝗼𝘄 𝗮𝗹𝗴𝗼 𝘀𝘁𝗿𝗮𝘁𝗲𝗴𝗶𝗲𝘀 𝗮𝗰𝘁𝘂𝗮𝗹𝗹𝘆 𝘄𝗼𝗿𝗸

- 𝗘𝗿𝗻𝗲𝘀𝘁 𝗖𝗵𝗮𝗻 𝗯𝗼𝗼𝗸𝘀 (𝗔𝗹𝗴𝗼𝗿𝗶𝘁𝗵𝗺𝗶𝗰 𝗧𝗿𝗮𝗱𝗶𝗻𝗴 & 𝗤𝘂𝗮𝗻𝘁𝗶𝘁𝗮𝘁𝗶𝘃𝗲 𝗧𝗿𝗮𝗱𝗶𝗻𝗴)
  These felt practical.
  They helped me think beyond “strategy ideas” and understand backtesting, risk management, overfitting, execution, and what it actually takes to move from idea → system.

𝟯. 𝗗𝗼𝗻’𝘁 𝗶𝗴𝗻𝗼𝗿𝗲 𝗱𝗲𝗿𝗶𝘃𝗮𝘁𝗶𝘃𝗲𝘀 & 𝗿𝗶𝘀𝗸

I underestimated this early on.
Bad idea.

- 𝗢𝗽𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻𝘀, 𝗙𝘂𝘁𝘂𝗿𝗲𝘀 𝗮𝗻𝗱 𝗢𝘁𝗵𝗲𝗿 𝗗𝗲𝗿𝗶𝘃𝗮𝘁𝗶𝘃𝗲𝘀 𝗯𝘆 𝗝𝗼𝗵𝗻 𝗛𝘂𝗹𝗹

Yes, it can feel intimidating, but this book quietly builds your finance intuition. At some point, you realise markets are basically pricing risk.

𝟰. 𝗧𝗵𝗲𝗻 𝗴𝗼 𝗱𝗲𝗲𝗽𝗲𝗿 𝗶𝗻𝘁𝗼 𝗠𝗟 𝗳𝗼𝗿 𝗳𝗶𝗻𝗮𝗻𝗰𝗲

A lot of people start here too early.
Fancy models won’t save weak foundations.

- 𝗔𝗱𝘃𝗮𝗻𝗰𝗲𝘀 𝗶𝗻 𝗙𝗶𝗻𝗮𝗻𝗰𝗶𝗮𝗹 𝗠𝗮𝗰𝗵𝗶𝗻𝗲 𝗟𝗲𝗮𝗿𝗻𝗶𝗻𝗴 — 𝗠𝗮𝗿𝗰𝗼𝘀 𝗟𝗼𝗽𝗲𝘇 𝗱𝗲 𝗣𝗿𝗮𝗱𝗼
  This one changes how you think about applying ML to markets. Feature engineering, labelling, backtesting… much more practical than generic ML content.

If I had to summarise the journey:
Understand markets → learn strategies → understand risk → add ML
Not the other way around.
